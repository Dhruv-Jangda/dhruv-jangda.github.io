Artificial Intelligence

As conceptualised by Turing Machine in 1936, if replies from a machine and human becomes indistinguishable, the machine is said to posess Intelligence, artificial, not human like real. So what is ML then? Nothing, but a set of mathematical algorithms that turn AI to reality.
Hence what some enthusiasts learn and study are Machine Learning algorithms and not AI. Most of the times AI and ML are used interchangably but ML is actually a subset of AI.

ML algorithms began getting published on paper right from 1950s in some mathematical forms, hence are not something new. Rather they have been coded, scripted and optimised in the form of packages in recent years such that they have become handier to use, if you know python, R or MatLab.

Being a person interested in learning and implementing such algorithms, I have worked on MXNet, MXBoard and Tensorboard and have also implemented some of the working projects. High level packages like Scikit-Learn provides an implementation of ML algorithms whereas MXNet is designed in such a way to implement any form of algorithms as per the way needed, thereby providing more control and flexibility over the algorithms. Hence, I still am learning MXNet and would love to use it as one single source of ML algos in future in my python projects. I also look forward to learning DL4J, that would provide better computation speed via distributed computing.


Analytics

Ever imagined, how a machine makes decisions? Using ML algorithms? Yes, but still it doesn't covers the complete picture. So, what is fed in those algorithms to make a machine decide? Nothing, but factors that convey some information.

As an example, a cricket match being played on a pitch that favours batting and on the ground with shorter boundaries, then one should place a bet on the team with better batting performance. But how do we know if a team is better in batting performance or a pitch is favouring batsmen or ground boundaries are shorter.

Crunching or discovering such information out from the available data is what is Analytics. Hence, after data cleaning this forms the preliminary stage of every data science problem and is the stage where 70%-80% of the time is spent throughout the solution and is also the stage that makes an algorithm accurate to correct decisions.

I tend to do most of the R&D during this stage and make the graphs of the available data and perform statistical analysis and hypothesis over the factors. Nothing is right or wrong in this stage, we just need to crunch out the relevant information and shortlist the potential factors.
